The rules for giving and receiving blood are as follows:
 
1. A person with antigen A may only give blood to another person with antigen A.
2. A person with antigen B may only give blood to another person with antigen B.
3. A person with the Rh factor may only give blood to another person with the Rh factor.
4. A person with none of the above factors (O-) can give blood to anyone.

ABO system
A, AB have antigen A
B, AB have antigen B
O, has no antigen

Rh system
Rh+, Rh antigens are present in their blood
Rh-, Rh antigens are not present in their blood

Donor Chart:
Blood Type          |Can give to        |Can receive from           |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
A+                  |A+, AB+            |A+, A-, O+, O-             |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
B+                  |B+, AB+            |B+, B-, O+, O-             |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
AB+                 |AB+                |Everyone                   |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
O+                  |A+,B+, AB+, O+     |O+, O-                     |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
A-                  |A+, A-, AB+, AB-   |A-, O-                     |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
B-                  |B+, B-, AB+, AB-   |B-, O-                     |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
AB-                 |AB+, AB-           |AB-, A-, B-, O-            |
---------------------------------------------------------------------
O-                  |Everyone           |O-                         |
---------------------------------------------------------------------


Write a function that takes in a donor's and receiver's blood types as strings and returns whether 
or not the donor can safely give blood to the receiver, according to the rules above.
